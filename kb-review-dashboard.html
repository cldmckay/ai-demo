A consistency, currency, and clarity audit of 21 IU Knowledge Base articles covering enterprise-supported AI tools, with per-article quality feedback and a research-grounded improvement plan.

Executive summary

The content is solid but drifting

Individually, most of these articles are accurate and readable — the REALLMS set and the newest teaching-tool pages are genuinely strong. The weakness is at the system level: the same facts are re-stated in many places and have quietly fallen out of sync (model names, data-classification wording, which tools are "recommended"), a recently-edited flagship article still carries 2021-era claims, and a scatter of broken links and copy-paste errors erode trust. These are governance problems, not writing problems — and they're fixable with single-sourcing, a shared template, and a review cadence.

Biggest themes

  • Inconsistent tool facts. The Microsoft 365 Copilot page says it runs on "ChatGPT-4" with a "2021" knowledge cutoff; the hub page says "ChatGPT-5o." Both can't be right, and both look wrong.
  • Duplication that drifts. Tool specs live both in the "Generative AI tools at IU" matrix and in each tool's own article, so they disagree over time (e.g., Copilot data classification: "University-internal" vs "Critical").
  • Stale claims. "IU is currently evaluating alternatives to the public version of ChatGPT" persists even though ChatGPT Edu is now offered.
  • Terminology drift. "REALLMS" vs "REALLMs," "NotebookLM" vs "Google Notebook LM," varied phrasings of the research-data carve-out.
  • Link rot & copy-paste errors. A truncated "KB00" link, three malformed mailto links, a protocol-less URL, and a NotebookLM page that signs off talking about "Google Gemini."
