Elements Demo Chatbot Evaluation ChatAIU response quality across three IU personas

This dashboard evaluates the Elements Demo chatbot (chataiu-stage.ea.iu.edu/elements-demo) against 150 questions — 50 each from the perspective of a new faculty member, a departmental administrative assistant, and an associate dean. Every question was scored on four dimensions, then asked live to the chatbot and graded on the quality of its answer versus the Elements website and its linked documents.

How to read this dashboard

Question scores (1–10, set before testing): Commonality (how often this audience asks it), Difficulty (how hard to answer well), Technical complexity, and Risk (harm from a wrong/confusing answer). Usefulness = round(0.35×Common + 0.30×Risk + 0.20×Difficulty + 0.15×Technical).
Response grades (from live testing): Clarity and Completeness (vs. the source data) are each 1–10; the letter Grade (A–F) reflects how well the answer met that persona's need. The bot is a retrieval system over the Elements site and cites a “Source” panel per answer. Click any row's ▸ arrow to read the verbatim response and evaluation notes.

Overall result

Across all 150 questions the chatbot earned a 3.34 GPA (A=95, B=34, C=8, D=3, F=10), with average clarity 8.1/10 and average completeness 7.2/10. The most common failure mode was a “I couldn't locate any data related to your query” deflection on topics the source actually covers (e.g., evaluation ratings, h-index, some reporting tools).

D43How do I interpret scholarly metrics like h-index, SNIP, and SJR in reports?4676588A
D43 — How do I interpret scholarly metrics like h-index, SNIP, and SJR in reports?
Common 4Difficulty 6Technical 7Risk 6Usefulness 5Clarity 8Completeness 8Grade A
Chatbot response (verbatim)
In Elements@IU reports, scholarly metrics are grouped as article, journal, and author metrics. H-index: Elements provides author metrics that include H-indices; interpretation details are in the “Understanding Scholarly Metrics & H-Index” guide. SNIP (Source Normalized Impact per Paper): Citations in the journal’s present year to publications from the past three years, divided by total publications in the past three years, adjusted to the citation norms of the journal’s discipline. SJR (SCImago Journal Rank): Journal influence in a subject category based on Scopus-tracked citations received and the influence of the sources of those citations; based on the Google PageRank algorithm.
